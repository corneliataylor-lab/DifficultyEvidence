External anchors integrate DHH-specific norms — SKI-HI LDS (Watkins et al. 2004/2020), VCSL (Simms, Baker & Clark 2013), ASL-CDI 2.0 (Caselli, Lieberman & Pyers 2020) — alongside CDI/Wordbank, ASHA, NELP, Brown, and developmental milestone research. Uncalibrated items show estimated δ with source badge. Age sub-bands anchored by VCSL (75% mastery), SKI-HI unit ranges, Wordbank/CDI medians, and DRDP transitions. Rebuilt against revised form text (Feb 2026).

Estimated δ methodology: Items without Rasch calibration use one of four estimation routes: X (cross-domain transfer) — same item text calibrated in R or E domain; δ transferred directly (strongest). I (form-peer interpolation) — bracketed between calibrated items with similar construct on same form. M (range midpoint) — midpoint of target/estimate range already present in source data. RD5
Analytical reading
 Retells a familiar story in the correct order, including main characters, setting, and key events. 4XX-27
LATE★★★
– SKI-HI U19, 48–54 mo, tells long stories. 'storytelling includes setting up people/objects in space' 50th %ile 4;5. Peterson & McCabe (1983): full retelling ~54–60 mo
– Note, age 6+, McCabe & colleagues found that around age 6, children produce coherent, structured personal narratives with orientation, complication, evaluation, and resolution (McCabe & Rollins, 1994; Peterson & McCabe, 1983)

ASL-specific: classifier constructions for depicting events not adult-like until ~age 8 (Supalla, 1982; Schick, 1990); role shift/constructed action develops through age 7 (Emmorey & Reilly, 1998). Note: hearing-normed 'retelling in order' research does not address ASL narrative conventions (spatial setup, role shift, line segmentation)
